Investment Philosophy

Investment Process

I invest in businesses rather than market narratives. My objective is to own high-quality companies that can compound intrinsic value over long periods while minimizing the risk of permanent capital loss.

Rather than forecasting macroeconomic cycles or short-term market movements, I focus on understanding how a business creates value, whether its competitive advantage is durable, and whether the current market price offers a meaningful margin of safety.

What I Look For

Every investment must demonstrate durable business quality supported by measurable fundamentals.

I prioritize companies with:

  • High and sustainable returns on invested capital (ROIC)
  • Strong free cash flow generation
  • Pricing power and resilient margins
  • Asset-light or capital-efficient business models
  • Disciplined capital allocation
  • Long-term reinvestment opportunities

Research Process

Investment ideas originate from a repeatable, bottom-up screening process.

My workflow follows five steps:

  1. Screen for financially strong businesses using quantitative filters.
  2. Evaluate competitive advantages, industry structure, and management quality.
  3. Estimate intrinsic value through conservative valuation and scenario analysis.
  4. Allocate capital based on conviction, valuation, and downside protection.
  5. Continuously monitor fundamentals and reassess the investment thesis.

Portfolio Construction

Not every investment deserves the same amount of capital.

Position sizing reflects business quality, valuation, conviction, and expected long-term risk-adjusted returns. Core holdings receive the largest allocations, while new ideas begin as smaller positions and earn additional capital only as conviction increases.

Sell Discipline

Every position continuously competes for capital.

I sell or reduce a position only when:

  • The thesis changes — The original investment case is no longer valid.
  • Valuation exceeds intrinsic value — Expected future returns no longer justify the risk.
  • A superior opportunity exists — Capital can be redeployed into a more attractive investment.

What I Avoid

I generally avoid businesses with:

  • Excessive leverage
  • Commodity-driven earnings
  • Weak pricing power
  • Structurally declining industries
  • Speculative narratives unsupported by free cash flow
  • Valuations that rely on overly optimistic assumptions
